What does it do?

It helps you create full color images using your monochrome camera and filters. You begin by specifying the desired integration time of the luminance filter. In response it calculates the integration time of each color filter so as to balance the signal-to-noise ratio over all channels. Optionally, you can select the amount of hardware binning, and the color balance ratios for your particular camera and filters.

What does it do?

Depending on your CCD/CMOS camera the image sensor responds differently to red, green, and blue light. For example the Atik 314E is most sensitive to green but least sensitive to red. The Altair 290M is most sensitive to red but least sensitive to blue. The purpose of color balancing is to discover the relative sensitivity expressed as ratios. We will use those ratios to determine proper exposure.

What does it do?

In order to perform the task of color balancing you need to select a star similar to our Sun, a spectral class G2V star. Sunlight is defined as white light. Using a G2V star for color balancing ensures that Sun-like stars in your astrophotographs will render anywhere from bright white to shades of gray.
