G2V Star Database

What does it do?

In order to perform the task of color balancing you need to select a star similar to our Sun, a spectral class G2V star. Sunlight is defined as white light. Using a G2V star for color balancing ensures that Sun-like stars in your astrophotographs will render anywhere from bright white to shades of gray.

Use the table below to choose the range of Right Ascension (RA) given the season. Data is grouped by northern hemisphere and southern hemisphere, and then sorted by RA. Scrutinize the Declination (DEC) field. Observers in extreme northern latitudes should favor highly positive DEC if possible. Observers in extreme southern latitudes should favor highly negative DEC if possible.

  • Northern Hemisphere observers:
    • Spring: RA from 9h to 15h
    • Summer: RA from 15h to 21h
    • Fall: RA from 21h to 3h
    • Winter: RA from 3h to 9h
  • Southern Hemisphere observers:
    • Spring: RA from 21h to 3h
    • Summer: RA from 3h to 9h
    • Fall: RA from 9h to 15h
    • Winter: RA from 15h to 21h
